Abstract

This workshop introduces the foundational principles of sustainable design in the built environment, examining the historical evolution of sustainability and the key drivers influencing current architectural and urban planning practices. Participants will explore environmental, social, and economic aspects of sustainability and learn how these interconnect through real-world examples and integrated design strategies.

Language: Persian

03

Brief

In this workshop, participants will:

  • Build a deep understanding of the dynamics and implications of sustainable development in built environments, including how global policies and local actions intersect.
  • Evaluate sustainability through the lens of economy, society, and environment using a triple bottom line perspective, recognizing trade-offs and synergies.
  • Study real-life projects to uncover best practices and common design pitfalls in sustainability implementation, with focus on context-sensitive strategies.
  • Gain technical skills in tools like EnergyPlus, Ladybug, and Honeybee to simulate energy and environmental performance across different climate zones and building typologies.
  • Apply learned strategies in practical exercises aimed at climate adaptation, user comfort, and ecological balance—emphasizing passive design and low-carbon materials.
  • Understand how human behavior and occupant comfort influence energy performance and spatial design, integrating behavioral simulation into environmental analysis.
  • Explore the role of traditional knowledge and cultural context in shaping sustainable solutions that are locally grounded and socially acceptable.
  • Develop workflows for integrating sustainability goals into design processes, from concept to operation, ensuring continuity between design intent and real-world outcomes.
  • Learn to critically assess sustainability certifications and frameworks (e.g., LEED, BREEAM, WELL) to determine their relevance and applicability.
  • Engage with emerging trends in regenerative design, including biomimicry, ecosystem services, and circular economy principles, to push beyond sustainability toward net-positive impact.

By combining analytical tools and sustainable thinking, attendees will be better equipped to lead the next generation of responsible and regenerative design practices.
