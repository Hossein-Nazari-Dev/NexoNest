Abstract

This workshop explores sustainability, its historical development, and multifaceted dimensions shaping sustainable design practices in architecture and urban planning. It covers environmental, social, and economic sustainability, examining their interdependencies through detailed case studies. Participants will also be introduced to essential tools for environmental analysis, including building energy simulations and climate-responsive design software, to establish practical and applicable workflows.

Brief

This workshop comprehensively covers sustainability principles and their practical application in architectural and urban design. Participants will:

  • Establish a strong foundational understanding of sustainability concepts, their origins, evolution, and significance in contemporary design.
  • Explore the triple bottom line approach deeply, evaluating the economic, social, and environmental impacts and benefits of sustainable practices.
  • Analyze detailed case studies showcasing successful sustainable design strategies and critically assess projects with unsustainable outcomes to avoid common pitfalls.
  • Develop proficiency in leading simulation and analysis tools like EnergyPlus, Ladybug, Honeybee, and climate-responsive design software, enabling precise environmental and energy analyses.
  • Engage in hands-on exercises to apply theoretical knowledge practically, fostering skills to create designs that address climate resilience, energy efficiency, cultural preservation, and community wellbeing.

Through combining theoretical knowledge and practical skills, attendees will be capable of designing resilient, equitable, and efficient projects adaptable to various contexts.
